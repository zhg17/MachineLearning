Does a fighters significant strike accuracy affect winning percentage¶

Another statistic looked at when matches are decided by judges decisions are significant strikes landed. These strikes differ from those counted in the total strikes landed because significant strikes are those which are landed from a distance when either in the clinch (when standing) and on the ground. Significant strikes tend to do more damage than regular strikes as the increased distance that the strike is landed from increases the force of the strike and in turn causes more damage to the opposing fighter. We wanted to make a predictive model by measuring the accuracy of a fighters significant strikes, measured as significant strikes landed over atttempted, and plotting that against their win percentage.

Feature selection¶

After removing non-numeric columns, our dataset still has 58 features. We will use feature selection to select the 20 most important features to improve the accuracy of our training algorithms.

To help in feature selection, we will implement Recursive Feaeture Elimination. The basic concept is to recursively construct a model, removing either the worst or best feature at each step. The newton-cg method is used for the solver.

The RFE method has selected the following 20 most important features out of the dataset: 'B_current_win_streak', 'B_avg_SIG_STR_pct', 'B_avg_SUB_ATT', 'B_avg_TD_att', 'B_total_title_bouts', 'B_win_by_KO/TKO', 'B_win_by_Submission', 'B_wins', 'R_avg_SIG_STR_att', 'R_avg_SIG_STR_landed', 'R_avg_TD_landed', 'R_avg_TD_pct', 'R_avg_TOTAL_STR_att', 'R_avg_TOTAL_STR_landed', 'R_losses', 'R_total_title_bouts', 'R_age', 'B_win_by_Decision', 'B_avg_TOTAL_STR_pct', 'R_avg_TOTAL_STR_pct'
